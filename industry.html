Industry Overview

The healthcare industry focuses on delivering patient-centered care through hospitals, surgical centers, clinics, and specialized practices. It combines skilled medical professionals with advanced technology to improve patient outcomes, safety, and access to care. Nursing plays a critical role in bridging medical knowledge with compassionate, hands-on patient care.

A Career I find Interesting

Role: Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetist (CRNA).
As a CRNA, I would provide anesthesia care for patients before, during, and after surgical and medical procedures. This role requires advanced clinical decision-making, attention to detail, and calm judgment in high-pressure situations. CRNAs work closely with surgeons and healthcare teams to ensure patient comfort, safety, and stability throughout procedures.

Potential Cybersecurity Issues

  • Patient data privacy: Unauthorized access to anesthesia records and EHRs could compromise patient confidentiality.
  • System downtime: Technology failures during procedures could delay care or impact patient safety.
  • Human error: Weak passwords or improper system use by staff can expose sensitive patient information.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Strong authentication: Use secure logins and multi-factor authentication for all clinical systems.
  • Training and awareness: Educate healthcare staff on recognizing phishing attempts and protecting credentials.
  • Access control: Ensure staff only access information necessary for their role.
  • Regular updates: Keep systems and devices patched to reduce vulnerabilities.
  • Secure data handling: Encrypt patient data and follow HIPAA privacy and security guidelines.
